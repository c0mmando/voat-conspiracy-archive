Part of me is upset about this and the way it's being done, but another part of me has seen that these regulatory bodies have been partially if not fully captured by the companies they're meant to regulate. The first Chief Administrator of the EPA was a Monsanto board member. https://muckrakingmaven.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/monsanto_federal_positions.png

Regulatory capture has been a long-time problem with US regulatory agencies, on both sides of the aisle: http://i.imgur.com/emAcFyM.jpg

Regulation is not bad of course, and is often very good and important, but if the governmental regulatory bodies are run by the same companies that the regulation is meant to protect us from, then it often works opposite to its intended goal, or may use the stated regulatory goal to consolidate an existing monopoly.

All that being said, the way Trump's administration is going about it is very ham-fisted and is causing all sorts of problems. The EPA isn't all bad by any means, but there is a serious problem with revolving door between industry and regulation, something which is banned in most developed countries. Although ultimately I doubt Trump will actually fix this root issue (despite playing off of it to gain support) and will probably only make it worse.
