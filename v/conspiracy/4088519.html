A useful perspective ( conspiracy )

submitted 2020-10-27T19:37 by uab

Our problem, in humanity, seems to me the fact that what we call psychopaths put up a false facade that is easier to believe, than is the actual truth about such people. There is a saying that goes approximately: smart people talk about principles, average people talk about events, less smart people talk about people.

If we increase the focus on the nature of conspiracists, that is the nature of people who are behind hidden agendas to exploit, oppress, torture and murder, I think we will move closer to setting things straight. Some of the big 'conservative' podcasts and so on, keep on documenting and hightlighting bad things that are being done by the psychopaths. Well, whereas documentation is certainly valuable, it seems to me almost the equivalent of being in a boxing match, and limiting yourself to counting how many times you're being punched in the face.

In the long run, documenting that psychopaths are engaged in conspiracies, is not going to accomplish anything, I suspect. The presumption seems to be that there are 'some among us' who did bad things, and got away with it, and that the solution is to document their trespass, and to then punish them. In my opinion, this is nonsense, like keeping on throwing water out of your leaking boat, but without actually fixing the leak...

What we should do, what I think is the eventual solution that is going to come sooner or later, is to study and learn about the different types of human that exists, to study the different types of personality, motivations, the different types of brain. Yes, some of these things show up in brain scans. Once we have established that, no, not all humans are the same, then we can start to arrange our societies accordingly.

We used to demand that left-handed people write with their right hand. That doesn't make sense, and so we eventually figured this out, and let them write with their left hand. Something similar can be done in the matter of views and values. Anyone who thinks it's strange how the world is organized, how anti-human it often seems, may receive a useful perspective from looking into the sorts of things I mentioned.

I noticed the topic of psychopathy, many years ago. Recently I've noticed an additional approach, namely something called evolutionary psychology. The first evolutionary psychology presentation that I noticed, and it was not the first time but a couple of years later when I came across it a second time, is the Stefan Molyneux video Gene Wars - Part 3. From there I continued on to reading the book he partly based it on, The Evolutionary Psychology Behind Politics. And now I have, very happily, also discovered Dr. Edward Dutton 'The Jolly Heretic' (pictured). Dutton livestreams on YouTube, and later removes the video, and instead keeps it on BitChute.

If you want a new, and in my opinion useful, perspective on subversion, oppression and hidden agendas against humanity, I recommend:

  • Gene Wars - Part 3, Stefan Molyneux

  • The Evolutionary Psychology Behind Politics, 'Anonymous Conservative', 2014

  • Dr. Edward Dutton 'The Jolly Heretic' on YT and BitChute
