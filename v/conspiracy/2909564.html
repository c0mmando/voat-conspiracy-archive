Does music from your hometown radio station sound better? ( conspiracy )

submitted 2018-12-13T09:53 by Diogenes_The_Cynic

I've been toying with this concept for a while.

Radio stations used to get super high quality magnetic tape way back in the day. The bosses would send them singles (never full albums) through regular mail, and expect each tape to get tons of airtime when its new. I dunno how long ago they went to digital, but the music out now sounds flatter.

When you reuse a magnetic tape it stretches slightly. And you couple that with tons of reuse, temperature differences, manufacturing defects, each one would eventually develop its unique sound on top of the song its playing. Thats the one version of any song you heard the most when you were younger, and the one you grew used to, not the one on the cd, or the one you hear at a different radio station today.

They went to digital, it'll never sound that good again, and your brain knows the difference even if you don't.
