  1. Probably a random set of characters probably 100+ characters

  2. I don't know of any of the programs but the only known way to open them without a key is to brute force unlock the file. So something like this 0000000000 0000000001 00000000002 00000000003 etc

  3. There is probably a checksum released by wikileaks to verify their integrity. Google "checksum check"

Not give you blue balls but cracking a key like that is going to take forever. To see how many possible combinations you have to take the number of possibilities 62 (just under and lowercase letters and numbers not including symbols) then take it to the power of how many characters you have in the key. So say we have 100 characters in the key take 62^100 and you have the answer of 1.73447861573683 * 10^179 of possibilites. This is discluding any symbols, foreign characters, etc. Supercomputers would take years and years to roll through the different key combinations. Plus it could be 150 characters or 1000 characters, something that throws us back to even more possibilites. Something you, I, and three letter agencies cannot crack.
