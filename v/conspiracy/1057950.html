"I've never given up anybody in my life. I'm not gonna put anyone in prison." (1:35:01) He also consistently talks about Oswald as support for the shooter, rather than the shooter himself.

The knoll shooter was probably someone he knew personally, that is still alive as of this recording, and is probably someone Files cared for on a personal level. His tells during his story all seem to center around the moments leading up to the final shot, and his trip back to the getaway vehicle. All times when the theoretical 2nd party would have been with him.

There's more to this story than he's telling, but I doubt its going to come out while the unnamed parties are still alive. If Files dies before these other parties, I suspect he will not leave any new info behind. He seems to feel strongly about protecting those still alive, and I don't think it's because he fears for his own life.
