The new chips in credit cards are being used to promote the use of cell phone payment ( conspiracy )

submitted 2016-07-17T19:07 by United_States_Gov

This is just a thought I had today at quick-trip. So you know those new RFID chips that every card is required to have now? Well if you have ever had to use one like I have you know it's a massive hassle. You stick the card into a slot at the bottom of the card reader, and it takes considerably more time to process payment than it used to from just swiping.

Well I think this is intentional in order to get people to start using mobile payment systems more. Now it's up to y'all to figure out why they want us to use mobile pay over card because I don't see any real downsides.

Some reasons why I think this is being done purposefully.

1. The chip slot is in a difficult place to see and access. This makes putting the card in the slot difficult.

2. The process takes sometimes 5x longer to complete even after the card has been inserted. Most of the time the customer first swipes, then is denied until then put the card in the slot.

3. The RFID tag doesn't really add much in the way of security to your card. In fact I argue it makes the card less secure and it can be more easily accessed by equipment.

EDIT: 4. Also, mobile pay is by far the fastest form of payment, however people don't use it because of social stigma.
