jervybingly 2017-09-29T01:28

The difference between Eastern Standard Time and GMT/UTC/Zulu is five hours. So, for example, 6PM Eastern Standard Time, would be 11PM GMT on the same day/date. At 7:01 PM EST, it would be 12:01 AM GMT, so one day later than the time on the East Coast.

According to official reports, the Sandy Hook shooting began at 9:35 AM EST on Dec. 14, 2012. The GMT time of the first shooting would then have been 2:35 PM Dec. 14. If the time stamp on surveillance cameras used military time and GMT, it would have been 1435-14-12-2012. So whether the cameras' timestamp used EST or GMT, both would have shown Dec.14 as the day shooting began. I am not suggesting a conspiracy, but only expanding on what the OP and captbrogers said.
