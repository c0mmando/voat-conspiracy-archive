pitenius 2015-12-14T23:27

Bill Cooper was a drunk. Fine. He had some faults. He also had some good insights. Occasionally, he promoted sources which might not have been the best. For example, he doesn't question the origin story of "The Report from Iron Mountain" too closely. At the same time, he did an absolutely brilliant Al-Jazeera type job of repeating the information as he found it. He gives the reader the complete document and let's the reader draw the conclusions. A lot of other authors would quote judiciously and fit the document to their perspective. I don't always believe his conclusions -- but he never said I had to. He focused on presenting information. In this, his approach is like WikiLeaks. I generally don't find him to be a waste of time. Special bonus: he called out Alex Jones as a bullshit artist and manipulator . I believe history is bearing that out. On the other hand, he drank a lot and that seemed to be a factor in his death. (For some reason, criticisms of Cooper always start with "William Cooper was a drunk." Fine. 1890 called -- it wants it's virtues back.) I can't quite judge what went down -- was he silenced or was he a cantankerous drunk who fought with a sheriff?

I'd take his material as a starting point for my own consideration. Cooper might have missed details about context, etc. but he has a nose for obfuscation. It's also telling to me how much debunking he's received. I'd say he was on to something -- whether he knew what that was or could separate the signal from noise is debatable. But his approach threatened people -- more than Marrs or Ventura. He's like Mark Lane in that regard.
