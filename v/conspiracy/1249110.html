Underground military bases ( conspiracy )

submitted 2016-08-24T17:36 by 7even6ix2wo

When I was young I was living in a suburban ranch development on the outskirts of Tucson and one summer my friends and I walked way out into the desert. There were a series of hills and we routinely went to the first one but this day we walked out to the third one. From there we could see a vast expanse of desert dotted with with small concrete bunkers. I found out later they were (decommissioned) nuclear missile silos for the very large minute man missiles and since there was very little concrete above ground, they must have been very deep. There were A LOT of them out there. So there was definitely an underground base there because there were no above ground structures anywhere. Just cactus and rocks and these squat concrete silos.

The existence of the underground infrastructure isn't a conspiracy theory, it is a fact. The conspiracy theory is only when you say if the entrance to the base was on Street A or if it was on Street B.
