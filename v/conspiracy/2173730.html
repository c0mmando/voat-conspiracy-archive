Abstract A solar cell using micro-antennas to convert radiation to alternating current and ultrahigh-speed diodes to rectify the AC can in principle provide extremely high conversion efficiencies. Currently investigated rectennas using metal/insulator/metal (MIM) diodes are limited in their RC response time and have poor impedance matching to the antenna. We have investigated a new rectifier, referred to as a geometric diode, which can overcome these limitations. The geometric diode consists of a conducting thin-film, such as graphene, patterned in a geometry that leads to diode behavior.
