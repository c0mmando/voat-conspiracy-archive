Classical economics has a problem called the St Petersburg Paradox, which is a gamble on a coin toss . The classical economist tries to calculate the fair entry bet for the gamble based on the probability of a single event. However, due to math, it's impossible.

So, the physicist comes in and says instead of looking at the probablity of the a single set of coin flips, look at what the average person will make from participating in the bets, and divide accordingly to get the fair entry bet price.

So instead of looking at the probability of random events, look at the random participants over time.
