pitenius 2016-03-08T11:34

Two posters have mentioned " the film ", presumably. I suppose it's alright. The book by John Keel is a classic of conspiracy literature. Although /u/ElspethTirel wrote that "conspiracy has become a catchall term", the book is not really a cryptozoology piece. It's much closer to a MIB story. Keel is really good at underlining the human element, too: it seems possible that everyone was engaging in some kind of pronoic mass delusion. Keel is a pretty good writer, and I think you owe it to yourself to start there. The people who want to boil his work down to a Loch Ness parallel (real or not???) are missing a lot.
