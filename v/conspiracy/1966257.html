NASA Images - Please debunk this! ( conspiracy )

submitted 2017-06-29T19:16 by Psalm144-1

OK, here are two "official" NASA images of Earth from Space that I am having a hard time with.

IMAGE #1 - http://images.gawker.com/1460964860810600770/original.jpg

Image #1 is from the Apollo 11 Moon Mission. This shows Earth from lunar orbit. In this image we see Earth off in the distance appearing similar in size to how we observe the Moon from Earths surface. The Earth appears relatively small, occupying a rather small portion of the visible "sky".

Now fast-forward to 2015....

IMAGE #2 - http://www.nasa.gov/sites/default/files/thumbnails/image/epicearthmoonstill.png

Image #2 is from NASA's newly launched "EPIC" space program. They release to the public a set of images of Earth from space. In this picture we observe Earth from (allegedly) ONE MILLION miles away! Image #2 shows what is obviously a radically different perspective of Earth from the moon. Based on the Image #2 visual, imagine being on the surface on the Moon and looking up into the "sky" to observe the Earth. According to image #2 the Earth should appear rather LARGE and would occupy a majority of the visible skyline.

My conclusion is that at least ONE of these images has to be fake. Please debunk and explain to me how both of these images could possibly be genuine (as NASA steadfastly claims). The images contradict each other on a massive level!

This. You cannot draw any conclusion from these images without knowing the focal length of the lens and the size of the sensor (or size of film in case of Apollo 11). With a camera you can make an ant look like a skyscraper and a skyscraper look like an ant.

To reliably compare two images side by side, they not only have to be taken with a lens that has the same focal length, but the sensor size also needs to be the same.
