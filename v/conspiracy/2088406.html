ardvarcus 2017-08-26T21:21

Well, let's see ...

You'll want to read the Hermetic books. There are a couple of versions readily available, as well as some other versions of individual works such as the Divine Pymander . I believe Meade did an edition of the Hermetic books, and there is the Scott edition, which is useful but a bit dry. Concerning the Gnostics, you'll want the Nag Hammadi Library which is a collection of Gnostic texts in English translation. The Greek neo-Platonists are an interesting study, in connection with Hermeticism and Gnosticism. I'd also suggest you look into the magical writings of the Graeco-Egyptians. There's a nice hardcover edition of the magical Egyptian texts. Budge did some pleasant works on Egyptian occultism and they are easy reading. I'd suggest you also look into the alternative gospels -- there are collections available.

I don't know that you'll find much useful about what you call the "cult of Saturn." It must be historically obscure, because until running across it on the Internet I've never encountered mention of it. Of course you will find lore on Saturn in the Golden Bough .
