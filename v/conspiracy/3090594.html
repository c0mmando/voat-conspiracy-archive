Since you asked and since propaganda is one of my favourite topics:

During WWII, the allies discovered that the most effective propaganda to affect German soldiers was that which was honest (even to the detriment of the allies occasionally), funny and separated them from their leaders. Political leaders, mind you, not command staff. When a battle went the wrong way for the allies, this was also mentioned with praise for the strong fighting will of the German soldier and his competent officers.

The moral is that people respond more to narrative than facts (so long as you're not outright fabricating easily debunked nonsense, that is).

Secondly, during the various leftist insurrections in Europe, the police discovered that the hardest groups to infiltrate were the affinity groups. This is where we got the idea of cells operating independently but with ephemeral ties upwards (but not sideways) in the hierarchy and operational decision-making rested with the cell itself to prevent usurpation further up in the hierarchy.

The moral of the story is that you need to know the people you're socializing with.

To do that, do what has worked elsewhere where this is a problem:

  • Have gun clubs. Don't just show up at a range. It needs to be a social activity for the whole family. Have a picnic or other outing or something and get to know other families involved. Appear harmless.
  • Make it a sport. Sports are ingrained in culture as a positive ideal and propaganda campaigns telling you that shooting is not a "real" sport or that it is somehow different will be met with hostility by the otherwise indifferent because we've seen similar attacks against sports that are now literally part of the Olympics. Appear harmless.
  • Have firearms conventions. Make them fun. Not cringy fun and full of fightin' words but educational. Have lectures about history, metallurgy, chemistry etc. Get kids involved but don't propagandize to them, let them come along naturally. Remember you're selling your hobby to otherwise indifferent people. Appear harmless.
  • Learn to hunt. Seriously. You don't need to hunt , just learn how to. Give your activity a veneer of hobby and give it more legs to stand on. People don't like it when government or special interests attack the hobbies of other people. Appear harmless. Make yourselves seem like the victims, not angry men who are yelling about "muh cold dead hands."
  • Have smaller clubs organized hierarchically. Clubs should be small groups of reasonably active people. So small that everyone is on a first-name basis with everyone else. So small that you can buy a beer for everyone involved.
