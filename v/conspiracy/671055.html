Controlled opposition in media: a discussion thread ( conspiracy )

submitted 2015-11-20T22:08 by deathcomesilent

For those unfamiliar with he concept of controlled opposition, here is a decent explanation I found (on urban dictionary of all places). " The best way to control the opposition is to lead it ourselves. " -Vladimir Lenin

It is important to understand that the concept of controlled opposition applies to both cyberspace and realspace.

A realspace example might be: a protest that is designed to polarize people, and ultimately, to fall short of the group's goals.

A cyberspace example might be: a news program that questions the establishment to a point, but pulls punches on the issues that really matter.

I'd like to talk about the media/cyberspace category. Obviously the mainstream media fits into this category, but I'm far more interested in the smaller outlets that falsely portray themselves as the voice of the free-thinker.

What outlets do you believe to be controlled opposition, and why do you think so?
